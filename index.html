Teaching Children STEM In Africa

African countries face significant obstacles in educating the youth in Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics. Today, Sub-sahara Africa has the largest number of out-of-school children in the world with 21% of primary school aged children and 73% of lower secondary school children are out of school. More than half of upper secondary school aged children in Sub Sahara Africa do not have access to education.

Harnessing the power of Africa's booming youth requires deep investment in reducing barriers to quality education and bridging gaps. One area where this is clear is Science, Techinology, Engineering and mathematics(STEM) education. STEM Education is the bedrock of innovative and highly productive future workforces, integral to the economic development of the continent.
